Rollback & Recovery

Rollbacks must be predictable, auditable, and quick. This playbook ensures every release has a known escape hatch and a path to permanent fix-forward.

Applies to: Prod/UAT releases Goal: Fast recovery

Prerequisites

  • All deployments reference artifacts by immutable digest or tag with provenance.
  • Previous stable versions are retained in the registry and deployment manifests.
  • Runbooks document rollback commands for each service and are tested quarterly.
  • Observability dashboards exist for release validation (golden signals + feature flags).

Rollback procedure

  1. Declare the incident, page the on-call, and halt further promotions.
  2. Identify the last known good artifact (tag or digest) from the release notes.
  3. Re-deploy the known good version using the same pipeline (no manual serverside edits).
  4. Validate health checks, logs, and customer impact; keep feature flags aligned.
  5. Open a follow-up ticket to fix-forward; hotfixes merge to mainline and are re-promoted.

Recovery patterns

Configuration-driven

Use feature flags or config toggles to disable risky capabilities without redeploying.

Immutable redeploy

Roll back to the last signed image, then iteratively roll forward once the fix is ready.

Traffic shaping

Use canary or blue/green to route traffic away from the bad version while validating the rollback.

Data recovery

Practice restoration from backups and migrations down scripts to protect customer data.
