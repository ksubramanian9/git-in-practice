Anti-patterns Behaviors that slow delivery or erode traceability

These are practices that repeatedly caused incidents, merge chaos, or deployment ambiguity. If you encounter them, stop and realign with the approved operating model.

Branching & Releases

  • Maintaining long-lived feature branches that skip rebasing and diverge from main.
  • Shipping from environment-specific branches (e.g., uat, prod) instead of tagged commits.
  • Cherry-picking fixes into multiple branches without closing the loop back to main.
  • Cutting releases from unreviewed commits or skipping CI to ‚Äúsave time‚Äù.
  • Maintaining multiple active release trains without resourcing to support them.
  • Force-pushing shared branches after reviews have started.

Reviews & Collaboration

Review pitfalls
  • Bypassing CODEOWNER review through ad-hoc merges.
  • Massive PRs with unrelated changes that cannot be reviewed effectively.
  • Relying on Slack approvals instead of recorded PR reviews.
Collaboration smells
  • Splitting ownership by environment rather than by domain.
  • Hiding breaking changes in ‚Äúrefactor‚Äù PR titles.
  • Skipping changelog updates or tagging strategy documentation.

How to Respond

Spot: Raise a comment in the PR and link to the relevant standard (branching model, release lifecycle, CODEOWNERS).

Correct: Pair with the contributor to re-cut the branch or PR; avoid shame‚Äîfocus on outcomes.

Prevent: Add linters or automation (e.g., branch naming checks, required labels) when the same anti-pattern recurs.

Rationale

Anti-patterns erode trust in the delivery process and slow teams down with avoidable rework. Calling them out early keeps the system predictable and auditable.
